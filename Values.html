Values

Our Guiding Values

The Six Common Values of American Citizens Institute. 


The American Citizens Institute exists as a vehicle for those who shall work to ensure a fair, suitable, and orderly balance between individual freedoms, and the imposition of government social control. Guided by principles of the highest integrity and worth, all work shall pay duty to matters of cooperation, fairness, proportion, and benefit. It is with unity and knowledge that this institute hopes to foster the concepts that will provide a foundation on which lives can be saved.


I. We are independent

American Citizen Institute has no division or lines drawn. We are not aligned with any organization, or outside cause. We have no targeted opposition, nor do we harbor any hidden agendas. Our approach and perception is placed outside of any specific culture, state, political alliance, or group. We keep a universal conscience toward the goodwill for all people.


II. We are peaceful

American Citizen Institute is opposed to illegal aggression, force, or violence, even when the ultimate intent is for an honorable purpose. We have no opposition or enemy. We are servants for peace and do not protest, complain, or point fingers. We exist merely to work alongside and subservient to all of those closely tied to the problems that we are working to resolve. We think killing is avoidable in almost all situations. We place human life above all things.


III. We are human

American Citizen Institute knows that as human beings our reasoning is a process, and that human perfection is impossible. Through the realization that within us we have challenges of our own, and by working to overcome those challenges, we think is the best way to come to qualified conclusions and solutions. Some of the ways we do this is by adhering to a neutral, fair, and universal perspective, thinking back as well as ahead, and by using empathy and tolerance as our guide. We keep goodwill and compassion at the forefront of our intentions.


 IV. We are loving

American Citizen Institute regards love as a necessary two-way ingredient for life that requires tolerant, empathetic, and compassionate action and interactivity. We renounce hatred and all other forms of excessive fear.


V. We are transparent

American Citizen Institute believes that freedom of information promotes progress, just as the concealment of the truth is restrictive. We support the carefully managed exchange of information for solidarity and the uniting of communities.


VI. We are free

American Citizen Institute holds that reservation of basic individual freedoms must be protected so that individuality of life may thrive. We believe that a universally-desired safe and harmonious community can only exist under equal protections under the rule of law, and that any deviation of that rule, or tolerance for deviation, whether by any person or institution, can only serve as a disruption of that peace.


These are the guiding values and principles of the American Citizens Institute. 



Real World Issues

The following is a list of important issues that currently challenge the peace, happiness, and the very lives of millions of our country's citizens. Yet there has been a general resistance to any significant acceptance of responsibility for them. Instead, a growing lack of compassion by those unaffected runs parallel to the surmounting risk and suffering by those inflicted by the symptoms of these issues. While American Citizens Institute has only limited resources that are currently targeting other specific needs, these are issues that we still hold with great concern and importance. In our commitment toward saving lives, it is our desire to someday have the ability to do more to address these grave matters. For now, the least we can do is recognize them.

  1. MENTAL HEALTH - There are tens of millions of citizens who are severely mentally ill in the United States. Nearly all of them are without treatment, a home, or any case management assistance. Our jails are populated with these individuals whose only crime is their illness in many cases. Inmates commonly hold the same regard for the mentally ill as they do sex offenders. Unfortunately, the mentally ill do not receive any special protections and commonly suffer harsh abuse at the hands of other inmates
  2. DRUG ABUSE - There is only one prominent care program available to substance abusers in our country. Its effectiveness is unknown. It is a program that is home derived, homegrown, and promoted by word of mouth only. The entire self-help program is contained in a few chapters of one book written eighty years ago by a man who received not one unit of education on the matter, and who was simply a sufferer of alcoholism. The program receives no public funding, and is unmonitored by government. Meanwhile, as an opioid epidemic is rising in our country, and drug overdose is a leading cause of death, that book remains as the leading effort to address the issue and the only treatment option available for most Americans that become drug or alcohol dependent. While it may be effective for a small percentage of the population, there is an exponentially larger portion of the afflicted population who are dying.
  3. FOREIGN POLICY - Our industrial military complex costs up to an estimated three trillion dollars each year. Most of that money is put toward foreign aggression while our protection within our homeland remains unchanged. War that is waged against weaker nations in volation of international law and without congressional approval often results in the death of innocent men, women, and children who have committed no foul against America. An estimated twenty million people have been killed by our military as part of the United States foreign policy since World War II.
